Indigenous Languages in Treaty 4 and Treaty 6 Territories

Saskatchewan Polytechnic campuses are located on Treaty 4 & Treaty 6 territories and the homelands of the Métis. These territories cover the homelands of several First Nations and the Métis. We respectfully recognize the Indigenous peoples of these lands as part of our ongoing commitment to good relations and a reconciled future. This web page will briefly examine some of the languages that were used.

Note that there is overlap in the Indigenous peoples that lived in the two treaty areas. The original Nations of Saskatchewan include: nêhiyawak* (Plains Cree), Nahkawininiwak (Saulteaux), Nakota (Assiniboine), Dakota and Lakota (Sioux), and Denesuline (Dene/Chipewyan). List based on Indigenous peoples of Saskatchewan, Indigenous Saskatchewan Encyclopedia.

*The term for Cree varies according to dialect. Plains Cree are nêhiyawak, Woodland Cree are nêhithawak and Swampy Cree are nêhinawak.

Current Population Demographics

The Indigenous population in Saskatchewan is growing. This growth is evident among young people where 46% of Indigenous individuals are under age 25, compared to 29% for the rest of the Canadian population. Métis self-identification is also contributing to the growing population.

In Saskatchewan, Indigenous peoples make up 15.6% of the provincial population.

In Saskatchewan, there are 157,740 Indigenous peoples of whom: 62% are Registered Indian, 6% are Non-Status Indian, 32% are Métis, 0% are Inuit, and 0% have other Indigenous identities.

Source: Government of Canada. (2013). Aboriginal demographics from the 2011 national household survey. Some Words in Indigenous Languages

The following list shows some common words in some of the Indigenous languages used in Treaty 4 territory, courtesy of the Regina Public Library YouTube channel.

  • Michif (Métis)
    • Hello - Taanishi
    • Thank you - Maarsii
  • Saulteaux
    • Hello - ānīn
    • Thank you - mīkwēc
  • Cree
    • Hello - Tānisi
    • Thank you - Kinānaskomitin
  • Dakota
    • Hello - hau or ho
    • Thank you - Ho pidamayado or hau pidamayaye
  • Lakota
    • Hello - Hau
    • Thank you - Pilamaya

Treaty Time Line

Treaties 4, 5, 6, 8 and 10 were negotiated in what is now Saskatchewan. They were agreements between the First Nations and the British Crown. These treaties are the foundation of Saskatchewan’s history. They established the relationship between the newcomers, settlers, and First Nations. These treaty relationships exist today between the Government of Canada and the First Nations peoples. These treaties are to last “as long as the sun shines, the grass grows, and the rivers flow.”

The Office of the Treaty Commissioner’s A Timeline of Events Leading to Treaties in Saskatchewan is a more detailed timeline of events leading to treaties in Saskatchewan.
